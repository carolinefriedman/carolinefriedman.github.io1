Honeycomb Classroom + Multi-Use Space:

Today, millions of honeybees are disappearing from the face of the Earth. Studies show that there is a strong correlation between modern food production, monoculture, and pesticides, with the decline of the honeybee population. Honeybees are architects themselves, and they construct their own homes using the hexagon structure repeatedly in their hives. My design explores how architecture reconnects people with the honeybee while promoting environmentally friendly behavior. I created a greenhouse, classroom, and multi-use outdoor space that takes up approximately 800 SF along Boulder Creek.The aim of my design is is to explore how architecture can create a space where people and honeybees can meet, strengthen the human/nature connection, and inspire people to take better care of our environment.
