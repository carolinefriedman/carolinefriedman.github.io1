Honeycomb Greenhouse:

Designing for people and place in a time of changing landscapes, cultural transitions and emergent climate patterns requires sensitivity to the consequences of human impact on the multiple systems supporting life. I designed a small intervention in an open space, located in Boulder, Colorado along Boulder Creek, that serves as a microcosm for the dynamic forces affecting the surrounding environment. The anatomy and role of honey bee inspired my design, and the growth and development of my intervention can be seen in the “Honeycomb Classroom & Multi-Use Open Space” tab.
